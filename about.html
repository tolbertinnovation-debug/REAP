Our story

Two decades of restoring hope in Liberia

REAP is a non-governmental, non-profit organization in Bentol City, Montserrado County, dedicated to empowering the young people most affected by Liberia’s civil wars.

Our mission

Tools, training and care that change the course of a life

Our mission is to provide the critical tools, educational resources, psycho-social support and practical vocational training needed to restore hope and empowerment for children, orphans and young adults impacted by the civil wars in Liberia.

We empower and equip them to become middle-class technicians — entrepreneurial, self-sustaining and productive members of their community and society. Since our establishment in 2003, REAP has provided livelihoods and training for many Liberians who are now positively impacting society. REAP currently operates with 18 full-time and 14 part-time staff across its administrative and academic cycle.

Our vision

A Liberia where every young person can build a dignified, self-reliant future.

We envision communities transformed from within — by young people who were once vulnerable and are now skilled leaders, providers and changemakers.

Founder & history

Born from a season of need — built to last

In the aftermath of Liberia’s civil wars, countless children and young adults were left without parents, schooling or a path forward. REAP was founded in 2003 to answer that need directly.

2003

REAP is established

Founded in Bentol City to care for and educate children and young adults affected by conflict.

Growth

Vocational training takes root

The REAP VTC Institute expands hands-on training so graduates can earn a living in skilled trades.

Today

A multi-program organization

Education, vocational training, leadership development and sustainability initiatives now work together, supported by 32 dedicated staff.
