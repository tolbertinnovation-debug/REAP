Who we are

Turning resilience into opportunity for Liberia’s young people

A non-governmental, non-profit organization located in Children Home, Bentol City, Montserrado County, Liberia, West Africa, with a mission to provide critical needed tools, educational resources, psycho-social support and practical vocational training needed to restore hope and empowerment for children, orphans, and young adults impacted by the civil wars in Liberia.

We empower and equip them to become middle-class technicians, entrepreneurial, self-sustaining and productive members of their community and the society. Since its establishment in 2003, REAP has provided livelihood and trainings for many citizens of Liberia that are currently impacting society. REAP currently has (18) full-time staff and (14) part-time staff in its administrative and academic cycle.

  • Empower & equip

    We help young people become skilled, entrepreneurial and self-sustaining technicians and leaders.

  • Whole-person support

    Education is paired with psycho-social care so healing and learning happen together.

  • Rooted in community

    Graduates return value to their families, communities and the wider society.

What we do

Programs that build lasting independence

Each REAP program tackles a different barrier young Liberians face — from skills and schooling to enterprise and well-being — so that progress is durable, not temporary.

Flagship Program

REAP Vocational Training Center Institute (VTCI)

Hands-on, certified training in market-ready trades that give graduates a clear path to employment and entrepreneurship.

  • Technical & vocational skills with practical workshops
  • Entrepreneurship and small-business readiness
  • Mentorship from experienced instructors

Early Childhood

VAT & Early Childhood Development (ECDC)

Foundational education and nurturing care that give the youngest children a strong, healthy start in life and learning.

  • Early literacy, numeracy and social development
  • Safe, supportive learning environments
  • Family and caregiver engagement

Leadership

Practical leadership and life-skills seminars that prepare young people to lead with integrity in their communities.

Our approach

From vulnerability to a self-sustaining future

We meet young people where they are and walk with them until they can stand on their own — and then lift others.

Reach

Restore hope & safety

Equip

Educate & train

Release

Empower & multiply
